AIMS--To determine how the microbiology laboratories of one region process serological requests from patients with suspected infectious illness, referred to as "clinical syndrome" type patients in this study; to consider areas where improvement in the associated serology service could be made. METHODS--A prospective two month collection of data on all serological requests from patients with suspected infectious illness was undertaken. A questionnaire on laboratory policies/procedures was also completed by the 10 departments taking part. RESULTS--Serology specimens from "clinical syndrome" patients accounted for 1-2% of total microbiology samples.
The lab is equipped with
  • 1.Automated ELISA system for almost about 50 different ELISAs.
  • 2.Automated culture, identification and sensitivity system for aerobic and anerobic bacteria.
  • 3.Automated Culture system for Blood.
  • 4.Immunofluorescence technology for autoimmune diseases.
  • 5.Special tests such as Westernblot for HIV, Quantiferon TB gold test, ANA profile.
  • 6.Complete Allergy panel for food and inhalation allergens.
The laboratory has two levels of Quality control measures in the department.
  • 1.ATCC strains are used for quality control.
  • 2.Three level Internal Quality control measures with strict adherence to CLSI guidelines.
  • 3.Inter Laboratory Quality Assurance Programs - ILQA.
