Specifically, in clinical medicine, histopathology refers to the examination of a biopsy or surgical specimen by a pathologist, after the specimen has been processed and histological sections have been placed onto glass slides. Histopathology (or histology) involves the examination of sampled whole tissues under the microscope. Three main types of specimen are received by the pathology laboratory. ... This type of material is usually liquid rather than solid, and is submitted for cytology rather than histology (see Cytopathology).
Histological Techniques. In order to study tissues with a microscope they must be preserved (fixed) and cut into sections thin enough to be translucent. The process of fixation is briefly described in the next section. ... Following fixation, blocks of tissue must be cut into thin sections.
